DHCP snooping#Concept of using one or more trusted ports, identified as having legitimate DHCP servers attached. As clients communicate on the network, the switch builds a "bindings table"—a database that lists the client MAC address, DHCP-assigned address, switchport, VLAN, and remaining DHCP lease time. The network switch then filters any DHCP server messages from untrusted ports in order to protect the integrity of legitimate DHCP servers and their operation.
